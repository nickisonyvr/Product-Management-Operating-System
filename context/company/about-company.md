## Company Overview

Safe Software is a Canadian software company founded in 1993 by Don Murray and Dale Lutz, born from the real-world challenge of exchanging data across incompatible systems during a geospatial forestry project in British Columbia. Thirty years later, Safe's FME Platform is trusted by 25,000+ organizations and 200,000+ users across 121 countries and 50+ industries — from government and utilities to telecommunications and construction.

Safe's flagship product, FME, is positioned as the "All-Data, Any-AI Platform" — a no-code data integration and ETL platform with unrivaled support for spatial data, 800+ built-in transformers, and connectivity across thousands of formats and systems. Safe is a certified B Corp (2024), privately owned, and recognized as a Gartner Magic Quadrant Niche Player and G2 Leader across 7 grids (Spring 2026).

## Mission & Purpose

Safe Software exists to help organizations do more with their data — a lot more. With a human-first philosophy, Safe believes that data powers the world and that the right tools put that power in the hands of the people who need it, without requiring them to write code or navigate complex infrastructure.

## ICP / Target Market

- **Primary**: Mid-to-large organizations (500+ employees) with complex, multi-format data integration needs — particularly where spatial/geospatial data is involved.
- **Industries**: Government (local, regional, federal), Utilities & Energy, Architecture/Engineering/Construction (AEC), Transportation, Telecommunications, Natural Resources/Mining, Public Safety, Education.
- **Buying Centers**: GIS teams, Data Engineering/IT, Enterprise Architecture, Operations teams managing asset or field data.

### Green Flags (Strong-Fit Customers)
- Organizations managing large volumes of geospatial or spatial data
- Teams running manual, repetitive data integration or ETL workflows
- Enterprises with many disparate systems that need to exchange data
- Organizations in regulated industries needing data governance and audit trails
- Customers on subscription licensing who want self-serve administration (FME Organizations)

### Red Flags (Poor-Fit Customers)
- Organizations with purely transactional, non-spatial data needs and existing mature ETL tooling
- Very small teams (under 10 people) without dedicated data or GIS functions
- Customers requiring real-time sub-second latency at extreme scale (e.g. high-frequency trading)

## Positioning

Safe Software competes in the data integration and ETL category, leading with its unrivaled breadth of spatial and enterprise data connectivity — a wedge no general-purpose ETL vendor has matched in 30 years. FME is positioned as the "All-Data, Any-AI Platform": the platform that connects any data source, transforms it to any requirement, and now orchestrates any AI model — all without writing code. Safe's B Corp certification and human-first values reinforce a brand that resonates strongly with mission-driven organizations in government, utilities, and public infrastructure.

### Key Differentiators
1. **Unrivaled spatial data support** — Deeper geospatial format coverage and transformation capability than any general-purpose ETL platform; the de facto standard for GIS data integration.
2. **Breadth of connectivity** — 800+ transformers and support for thousands of formats and systems, from legacy enterprise databases to modern cloud platforms and AI services.
3. **No-code platform** — End-to-end data integration without writing code, making powerful ETL accessible to GIS analysts, data professionals, and operations teams.
4. **AI-ready** — MCPCaller Transformer, MCP server builder, and AI Assist position FME as the orchestration layer for enterprise AI workflows on top of existing data infrastructure.
5. **Trusted & proven** — 30+ years, 25,000+ organizations, Gartner Peer Insights Customers' Choice (only North American vendor, 2023), G2 Leader 7x, SOC 2 Type 2, ISO 27001.

## Product Portfolio

| Product | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **FME Form** | No-code desktop authoring tool for building data integration workflows using 800+ transformers across thousands of formats and systems. |
